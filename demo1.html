1. Weeping Fig

The weeping fig (Ficus benjamina) is a popular indoor tree known for its elegant, drooping branches and glossy green leaves. While it is a beautiful addition to homes, it is notoriously sensitive to environment changes and will drop its leaves if stressed

2. Tomato

The tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is a widely cultivated plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is an annual plant that can grow up to 3 meters tall and is commonly grown as a vegetable in gardens and farms.

3. Oliander

The oleanander (Olea europaea) is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae. It is a small evergreen tree that is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used to make olive oil.

4. Senna Plant

The senna plant ( Cassia senna) is a shrub in the family Fabaceae, native to the Middle East and northeastern Africa. It is cultivated for its leaves, which are used as a laxative and for other medicinal purposes.

5. Moss Ross

The moss ross (Moss Ross) is a type of succulent plant that is known for its attractive, fuzzy leaves and ability to thrive in various conditions.

6. Indian Pitcher Plant

The Indian Pitcher Plant (Nepenthes khasiana) is a carnivorous plant species found in the Eastern Himalayas. It is known for its distinctive pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects.

7. Golden Pothos

The Golden Pothos (Epipremnum aureum) is a popular indoor plant known for its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with yellow or white variegation. It is easy to care for and thrives in low to moderate light conditions.

8. Common Lantana

The common lantana (Lantana camara) is a flowering plant in the family Verbenaceae. It is a shrub that is native to the Americas and is widely cultivated for its colorful flowers and aromatic leaves.

9. Chinese Hibiscus

The Chinese Hibiscus (Hibiscus rosa-sinensis) is a tropical flowering plant known for its large, showy flowers that come in various colors including red, pink, white, and yellow.

10. Aloe Vera

The Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) is a succulent plant species known for its thick, fleshy leaves that store water and contain beneficial compounds.

11. Ajwain

The Ajwain (Carum copticum) is an annual herb in the family Apiaceae. It is native to the Middle East and northeastern Africa and is cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a spice and for medicinal purposes.

12. African Marigold

The African Marigold (Tagetes erecta) is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is a tall, robust annual that is widely cultivated for its colorful flowers and aromatic leaves.
